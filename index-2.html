Research Areas

Three Pillars of
My Research

My work spans three interconnected themes — each shedding light on how the brain shapes behavior in health and disease.

01 ──
🧠

Social & Emotional Behavior

How do adolescent brains regulate social, emotional, and addictive behaviors? Using the medial prefrontal cortex as a key node, I investigated how oxytocin modulates fear memory, anxiety, and sex-differential responses in adolescent rodents.

mPFC Oxytocin Fear Memory Sex Differences Adolescence Social Buffering
02 ──
⚡

Pain & Sensory Circuits

Characterizing direct synaptic connections between peripheral sensory and central neurons in orofacial pain (migraine/TMD) and psoriatic itch. I mapped IL-17R/ERK/TRPV4 and LPA/LPAR/PIEZO2 signaling pathways driving chronic pain states.

Migraine Orofacial Pain Psoriatic Itch TRPV4 PIEZO2 TMD
03 ──
🤝

Autism & Social Enrichment

My current postdoctoral work at Duke (Tim Dunn Lab) examines developmental changes associated with social enrichment and the neural mechanisms underlying social behavior in autism spectrum disorder — bridging behavioral and circuit-level analyses.

ASD Social Enrichment Neural Development Behavior Duke BME
